🎥 Screen Triggers
Know what’s ahead. Watch with confidence.
Screen Triggers is built on a simple but powerful idea: no one should have to be blindsided by content that could be distressing. Screen Triggers is a community library of content warnings for films and TV shows. Browse by title to see where potentially triggering scenes occur and what they involve.

When someone takes a few minutes to submit a trigger, they are helping:

▪ a survivor avoid unexpected trauma reminders
▪ a parent screen content before family movie night
▪ someone navigating grief, anxiety, or PTSD feel more prepared
▪ a viewer decide when they’re emotionally ready to watch something

Contributors turn passive viewing into a supportive, informed experience. They transform entertainment into something safer and more accessible for people with real-life sensitivities.

Because Screen Triggers is community-driven, its strength grows with participation. The more people who contribute, the more accurate, detailed, and representative the database becomes. Different people experience stories differently - and that diversity of perspective is what makes the platform meaningful.

Each submission says: “I noticed this. I care. I want someone else to be prepared.”

By contributing, users help build a culture of transparency around media - one where emotional wellbeing is respected, and where viewers can engage with stories on their own terms.

About This Project

Trigger Library was created to help viewers navigate potentially triggering content in movies and TV shows. By collecting community-submitted notes about specific scenes, we aim to give people more context before they press play.

This project is community-driven: entries are written by volunteers who share what stood out to them. It is not a complete or definitive record of every trigger in a title, and different people may experience the same scene very differently.

Nothing on this site is a substitute for professional mental health care or crisis support. If you are struggling, please reach out to a trusted professional, support line, or community resource in your area.

We hope this library can be one small tool to identify potentially distressing scenes in films and shows to support safer, more informed viewing for everyone.
